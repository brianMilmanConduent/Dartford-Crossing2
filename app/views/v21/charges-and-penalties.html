Dartford Crossing charges and fines

You must pay the charge if you use the Dartford Crossing between 6am and 10pm, or you may get fine (Penalty Charge Notice). Charges apply every day - including weekends and bank holidays.

How much you pay to cross depends on the type of vehicle you're driving and if you already have an account.

Exemptions for drivers with disabilities

You do not need to pay the charge if you get free UK vehicle tax because of a disability.

Your vehicle will be checked automatically when you make a crossing - you do not need to do anything.

Find out how to get free vehicle tax if you're a driver with a disability.



Local resident discount

You can apply for a crossing discount if you pay Council Tax to Dartford borough council or Thurrock council. Find out who your local council is.

You can either:

  • pay £20 a year to use the Dartford Crossing as many times as you want
  • pay £10 for 50 crossings, plus 20p for each additional crossing

You can apply for a local resident discount:

  • online
  • by post
  • by calling the Dart Charge contact centre


Dart Charge fines

If you do not pay the Dart Charge by midnight the day after you cross you may get a £70 fine (Penalty Charge Notice).

You must pay the fine within 28 days of receiving the notice.

The fine will be:

  • reduced to £35 if you pay within 14 days
  • increased to £105 if you do not pay it
  • you will also have to pay the original crossing charge

You can pay or challenge the fine online or call the Dart Charge contact centre.
